# JanSetu AI — Project Write-up (for submission portal)

**Track 1: People's Priorities — AI for Constituency Development Planning**

₹1,729 crore of MPLADS funds lie unspent nationally, only about half of released funds have been utilised since 2019, and 62% of MPs miss the mandatory 22.5% SC/ST-area allocation — not because money or need is missing, but because the *decision* between them is hard. Existing systems collect complaints (CPGRAMS, Swachhata) or track works after sanction (e-SAKSHI); none helps an MP's office decide which works to sanction. JanSetu AI is that missing decision layer. Citizens report issues and suggest works inclusively — by photo (Gemini Vision auto-classifies category, department, severity), by voice in 12 Indian languages with spoken responses (no literacy or typing required), or text — and it works offline, queuing reports on-device until signal returns. The office asks questions in plain language in any Indian language; Gemini converts them to guard-railed SQL over grievances, ward demographics, and the live MPLADS fund ledger, answering with charts, a demand-hotspot map (Google Maps), and statistical spike alerts.

The heart of the platform is the Priority Works briefing: Gemini fuses citizen demand signal, anomaly spikes, ward demographics (population, SC/ST share, schools, PHCs), and the fund ledger into a ranked list of costed, sanctionable, guideline-compliant works — flagging SC/ST-mandate gaps and drafting the formal recommendation letter to the District Authority in one click. Built on Gemini 2.0 Flash, FastAPI on Cloud Run (asia-south1), Google Maps Platform, and a documented SQLite→BigQuery scale path, one deployment serves a constituency of ~25 lakh citizens and onboarding a new constituency takes under a day. Privacy is by design: no Aadhaar or personal identifiers, consent screen, India data residency, and advisory (never autonomous) AI recommendations.
